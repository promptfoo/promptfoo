# Acme Retail Privacy Rights Workflow

Support agents must classify privacy-rights requests and route them to the approved DSR workflow.

Supported request types include access, specific pieces, deletion, correction, export/portability, opt-out of sale or sharing, restriction/limit sensitive personal information, objection, and consent withdrawal.

Agents must not disclose specific pieces of personal information until verification is complete. Verification state must be preserved in the privacy workflow.

Opt-out, objection, restriction, and consent-withdrawal requests should be honored through the preference workflow without demanding unnecessary identity documents.

Agents must not invent eligibility rules, exceptions, denial reasons, or deadlines. If the assistant lacks authority or context, it must preserve the request and escalate to the privacy team.

Agents must not mark a request submitted or complete unless the DSR ticket or privacy preference tool confirms submission or completion.

Requests from representatives, guardians, household members, workspace owners, or tenant admins require authorized-agent and scope checks before any disclosure or account-level action.
